Understanding Sleep Cycles

Stage 1: Light Sleep

The transitional phase between wakefulness and sleep. Your muscles relax and your brain produces alpha and theta waves.

Stage 2: True Sleep

Your brain produces sudden increases in wave frequency called sleep spindles. Body temperature drops and heart rate slows.

Stage 3: Deep Sleep

Delta waves begin. This stage is crucial for physical recovery, immune system strengthening, and growth.

REM Sleep

Brain activity similar to wakefulness. Dreams occur, muscles are paralyzed, and memory consolidation happens.

Complete Sleep Cycle: 90-110 Minutes

Tips for Better Sleep

Consistent Schedule

Go to bed and wake up at the same time every day, even on weekends.

Optimal Temperature

Keep your bedroom between 60-67°F (15-19°C) for optimal sleep.

Digital Sunset

Avoid blue light from devices at least 1 hour before bedtime.
